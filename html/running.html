  • Workout 1
    Long-Distance Workout

    This drill helps you run over a long distance while pacing yourself

    1. Warm-up: 5 minute easy jog
    2. Work interval: 5 minutes at comfortable pace
    3. Recovery interval: 1 minute at easy/slow pace
    4. Work interval: 4 minutes at comfortable pace
    5. Recovery interval: 1 minute at easy/slow pace
    6. Work interval: 3 minutes at comfortable pace
    7. Recovery interval: 1 minute at easy/slow pace
    8. Work interval: 2 minutes at comfortable pace
    9. Recovery interval 1 minute at easy/slow pace
    10. Work interval: 1 minute at hard (sprint) pace
    11. Recovery interval: 1 minute at easy/slow pace
    12. Cool down: 5 minutes easy jogging

    Source: https://www.verywellfit.com/effective-30-minute-running-workouts-2911891

  • Workout 2
    Sprint Workout

    Perform sprint workout routines three times a week with plenty of rest between workouts. Because of the intensity of these workouts, most athletes shouldn't do sprint workouts more than three times a week.

    1. Warm up. Before sprints, warm up thoroughly with easy exercise for 5-10 minutes. Perform the same exercise you will be using for your sprints.
    2. Sprint. Perform your first sprint at about 60 percent max intensity. If you feel any muscle tightness or joint pain, back off and continue to warm up.
    3. Recover. Recover for 2 minutes by slowing to a comfortable pace, but keep moving. This can be an easy jog or a walk, depending on your fitness.
    4. Sprint. Perform your next sprint at about 80 percent max intensity.
    5. Recover. Recover for 2 minutes.
    6. Sprint. Perform the remainder of your sprints at 100 percent max intensity or all-out efforts of 30 seconds. You should be pushing yourself to the max for each one.
    7. Recover. Recover for 2 to 4 minutes after each sprint to allow your breathing and heart rate to slow to the point that you can hold a conversation without gasping.
    8. Repeat. Repeat the sprint/recovery routine 4-8 times depending on your level and ability. For your first workout, you will want to stop at 4 sprints. That's fine. Try to build up to 8.
    9. Workout Goal. The goal is to do this workout six times in two weeks and then back off to twice a week for maintenance for six to eight weeks before you change your workout.
    10. Rest and Recovery. Allow at least one to two days of rest or another easy exercise between sprint workouts.

    Source: https://www.verywellfit.com/get-fit-faster-with-30-second-sprints-3120562
